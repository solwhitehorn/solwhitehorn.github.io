### A little article about the setup I’m currently using to publish articles on the go

------

I want to share with you on how I’m using my iPad to publish article on this website. At first I was using my personal laptop but I didn’t have it with me at all times. What I do have is an iPad that serves me very well for work. I was looking for a way to write up articles, push them on github and have them published when I’m on the go. Here’s how I do it at the moment.

**I. The Hardware**

I’m using a 9.7“ iPad that I got in 2018. It’s a good piece of equipment and even If I’m not too found of the Apple ecosystems, I got to admit that it does the job. At first I was mainly using it for work as I used to travel a lot and assist many meetings. I used to have my laptop with me all the time but it was more and more of a chore, then I went back to the good old times of paper notes. The good thing with paper is that it’s a quick way to note ideas and make some schematics, but if it's lost, there is no cloud to save you. Then came the iPad and the possibility to take handwritten notes easily converted as text with figure and all and saved as a word or pdf document.

I recently bought a small foldable Bluetooth keyboard from [Jelly Comb](https://www.amazon.com/Bluetooth-Keyboard-Jelly-Comb-Rechargeable/dp/B01LS32D52/ref=sr_1_10?crid=20EK0XR44250Y&dchild=1&keywords=jelly+comb+wireless+keyboard+for+ipad&qid=1599217104&sprefix=jelly+comb+wireless+keyboard+%2Caps%2C238&sr=8-10). It’s not the best keyboard in the world but it’s small, light and has all of the iOS shortcuts plus a little trackpad that I must admit can be quite useful. Using a keyboard with your iPad also have some great new way to use you tablet like alt-tabbing or controlling music playback with a single shortcut.

And then I have the Apple Pencil, the $99 piece of plastic that does a great job for handwriting but that is way overpriced. It’s the first generation which IMO is badly designed, especially the round shape and slippery plastic. Plus its recharge design of plugging it in the iPad is truly an nightmare.

**II. The Softwares**

I have several softwares that I use daily, for work or for this little blog:

- [Nebo](https://www.nebo.app) is a great app for handwriting notes. Its text converter is amazing and transforms my ugly handwritten notes to text. I our case here I use it to draft the plan of an article or just take notes. I also use it a lot when I’m gaming and want or need to take notes on stuff I want to remember.
- [iA Writer](https://ia.net/writer) is another great app that let you write directly in Markdown and has a lot of prebuilt shortcuts for an easy access to markdown formatting. You have a lot of options for syncing your documents with your typical providers. The interface let you focus on what you are currently writing and even analyze the style of your writing and suggest modification for a better article. A blessing for someone that is not an English native but still want to publish in English. It also gives you stats on words, characters and time average time to read. And a thing I discovered is the quick shortcuts menu that you can access or display with the command key. I’m currently writing this article on this app.
- [Working Copy](https://workingcopyapp.com) is simply the best github client that you can find, at least on the iPad. Lots of option to sync your files. Easy to use for just your code, .md files or whatever you need to push to or fetch from your repo. You can clone, you can edit, you can commit. You can do about everything that you need with this app. 

The way I do it right now is: I have a sync folder on my iPad where every edits or new file I make on iA Writer is automatically updated on Working Copy. I then launch Working Copy, commit my changes, and push it to github. And voila, it’s done. The good part is that I always have a hard copy of the website on my iPad if I’m not online. I don’t have to use cloud services like Dropbox or such. I’m gaining a lot of time and all of these softwares are easy to use. The only drawback that I can think about is that both iA Writer and Working Copy are paid apps while on my laptop I could do all of that for free.
